Spanish Trade Delegation Visit

March 2024 • Hong Kong

Meeting with Spanish trade representatives to explore premium spirits opportunities. The delegation included representatives from key Spanish wine regions who presented their premium offerings to United Spirits' procurement team.

← Back to Events

Event Overview

This significant visit by the Spanish Trade Delegation focused on exploring opportunities for Spanish wine and brandy producers in the Asian market. The delegation, representing the Spanish Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food, included representatives from key Spanish wine regions including Rioja, Ribera del Duero, and Priorat, as well as representatives from the Sherry Triangle region. The meeting was hosted at United Spirits' Hong Kong headquarters and facilitated valuable connections between Spanish producers and Asian distribution channels.

Key Highlights

The delegation presented comprehensive overviews of Spain's diverse wine regions, highlighting the unique terroir characteristics and production methods that distinguish each area. United Spirits' procurement team shared detailed market insights on consumer preferences in Asia, including packaging preferences, price points, and flavor profiles that resonate with Asian consumers. The discussion focused on identifying opportunities for premium Spanish wines and brandies in the Asian market, with special attention to the growing demand for aged spirits and limited edition releases. The meeting also covered potential collaborations for joint marketing initiatives and educational events to build awareness of Spanish wine regions among Asian consumers.

Featured Products

The presentation highlighted several premium Spanish products that show strong potential for the Asian market:

  • Corazón de Lolita Premium Coupage - A distinctive Spanish red blend with international appeal
  • Bernard Magrez Le Symbole 2020 - Premium Spanish wines from renowned Bordeaux producer
  • Honney Conti Napoleon VSOP - Spanish brandy with potential for premium positioning
  • Chateau Cabannieux 2019 - White wines with crisp acidity suitable for Asian cuisine pairing

Future Initiatives

Following the productive discussions, United Spirits has committed to increasing its Spanish product portfolio by 25% over the next year. The company will establish a dedicated Spanish Wine & Brandy section in its flagship tasting room, featuring rotating selections from different Spanish regions. Additionally, United Spirits will collaborate with the Spanish Trade Office to organize a "Spain Wine Month" in Hong Kong, featuring masterclasses led by visiting Spanish winemakers. A joint delegation visit to Spanish wine regions is also planned for Q1 2025, with the goal of strengthening relationships with producers and identifying new premium offerings for the Asian market. The partnership will also develop educational materials specifically for Asian consumers about Spanish wine regions and varietals.
