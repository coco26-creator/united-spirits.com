Education Event

The Art of Aged Whiskey

June 2025 • Hong Kong

Masterclass exploring the science and artistry behind whiskey maturation processes. Led by a renowned master blender, this session delved into the impact of cask type, climate, and time on flavor development.

← Back to Events

Event Overview

This exclusive masterclass was held at United Spirits’ tasting room in Central Hong Kong. Attendees, including collectors, distributors, and enthusiasts, learned about the intricate process of whiskey aging. The master blender shared insights from decades of experience, explaining how factors like wood type, warehouse location, and climate influence the final product.

Key Highlights

The highlight was a comparative tasting of Sunny Land Single Malt aged in different casks: ex-bourbon, sherry, and virgin oak. Guests could clearly taste the impact of each cask type. The master blender also demonstrated how to read a whiskey’s color and viscosity to predict its flavor profile. The session concluded with a Q&A and a signing of limited-edition bottles.

Featured Products

The tasting featured these expressions:

  • Sunny Land Single Malt S25 (Ex-Bourbon Cask) - Vanilla, oak, and caramel notes
  • Sunny Land Single Malt S25 (Sherry Cask) - Rich, fruity, with hints of dried fruit and spice
  • Sunny Land Single Malt S25 (Virgin Oak Cask) - Bold and spicy, with strong wood influence

Future Initiatives

United Spirits will launch a “Whiskey Academy” series, with monthly masterclasses on different aspects of whiskey production and appreciation. The next session, “The Science of Peat,” will explore the role of peat in Scotch whiskey. A digital “Whiskey Aging Simulator” will also be developed, allowing users to virtually experiment with cask types and aging conditions.
