Scotch vs Bourbon

April 2024 • Hong Kong

Comparative tasting session exploring the distinctive characteristics of these two iconic whiskey styles. Led by two master distillers, the session compared production methods, flavor profiles, and cultural significance of Scotch and Bourbon whiskeys.

← Back to Events

Event Overview

This educational event provided a comprehensive comparison between Scotch and Bourbon whiskeys, highlighting their historical origins, production methods, and flavor profiles. Led by two master distillers (one specializing in Scotch, the other in Bourbon), the session explored how factors like water source, grain selection, distillation techniques, and barrel aging create distinctive characteristics in each style. Participants engaged in a blind tasting to develop their ability to identify key flavor markers that distinguish these two beloved whiskey traditions.

Key Highlights

The session featured side-by-side comparisons of production methods, including differences in water sources, grain bills, fermentation processes, distillation equipment, and aging requirements. Participants learned about the geographical indications that define each style and how regional characteristics influence flavor profiles. The master distillers explained the aging process in different barrel types (ex-bourbon for Scotch, new charred oak for Bourbon) and how this impacts flavor development. The blind tasting component challenged participants to identify key characteristics of each style without visual cues, enhancing their sensory evaluation skills. The session concluded with a discussion on how to properly serve and enjoy each whiskey style for optimal appreciation.

Featured Products

The tasting session included a comparative selection of premium whiskeys:

  • Sunny Land Single Malt S25 (Scotch) - A 25-year-old Speyside single malt showcasing traditional Scotch characteristics
  • Caribbean Blaze Bourbon 46 (Bourbon) - A 12-year-old Kentucky bourbon demonstrating classic Bourbon profile
  • Sunny Land Single Malt France (Scotch) - A single malt finished in French oak, showing regional variation
  • BF Kingdom BE708 Wood Box (Scotch) - A premium blended Scotch with complex flavor profile
  • Caribbean Blaze Bourbon 18 Box (Bourbon) - A small-batch bourbon with distinctive character
  • Sunny Land Single Malt Sherry Cask (Scotch) - A single malt matured in ex-sherry casks

Learning Outcomes

Participants developed the ability to distinguish between Scotch and Bourbon based on sensory characteristics rather than labeling. They learned the historical and regulatory differences that define each category and how these impact production choices. The session provided practical knowledge for selecting whiskeys based on personal preferences and for different occasions. Attendees gained insights into the aging process and how different barrel types contribute to flavor development in each style. The event concluded with a detailed comparison chart that participants could reference for future whiskey selections, along with personalized recommendations based on their tasting preferences identified during the session.
